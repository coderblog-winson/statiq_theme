## 2026 Dinnerware Buying Guide: What You Need to Know

Before you click "Buy," here is a quick guide to understanding the materials.

### 1. Earthenware vs. Stoneware vs. Porcelain

* **Earthenware:** Fired at lower temperatures. It has a rustic, thick look (like terra cotta) but is porous and prone to chipping. Good for casual use.
* **Stoneware:** Fired at high temperatures with glass added to the clay. It is waterproof, very durable, and retains heat well. (e.g., Le Creuset).
* **Porcelain:** Fired at extremely high temperatures. It is white, non-porous, and very hard. It looks delicate but is tougher than stoneware.
* **Bone China:** Porcelain mixed with bone ash. It is the strongest material, lightweight, and translucent.

### 2. How many place settings do I need?

The golden rule is: **Number of family members + 4**.
If you are a family of 4, buy 8 settings (two boxes of 4-piece sets). This covers you when guests come over or when you haven't run the dishwasher yet. For holidays, 12 settings is the standard.

### 3. Open Stock vs. Boxed Sets

* **Boxed Sets:** Usually cheaper. Typically include a dinner plate, salad plate, bowl, and mug.
* **Open Stock:** You buy pieces individually. This is more expensive but allows you to skip the mugs (which many people don't use) and buy extra pasta bowls instead.

### 4. Current Trends in 2026

* **Pasta Bowls (Blates):** The low, wide bowl that doubles as a plate is the "it" item of 2026. Look for sets that include these instead of traditional deep cereal bowls.
* **Matte Finishes:** High gloss is out; satin and matte textures are in.
* **Mix and Match:** Don't be afraid to buy dinner plates in white and salad plates in a bold color.
